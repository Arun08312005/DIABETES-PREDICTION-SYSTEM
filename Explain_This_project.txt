üî¨ SCIENTIFIC PRINCIPLES BEHIND EACH FEATURE

Glucose (Primary Predictor)
Physiology: Blood sugar regulation involves pancreas (insulin), liver (glucose production), muscles (glucose uptake)

Pathology: Insulin resistance ‚Üí cells ignore insulin ‚Üí glucose accumulates ‚Üí pancreas overworks ‚Üí beta-cell exhaustion

Threshold Science: Fasting glucose >126 mg/dL diagnostic; 100-125 = pre-diabetes

BMI & Body Composition
Adipose Tissue Dynamics: Fat cells (especially visceral) secrete inflammatory cytokines (TNF-Œ±, IL-6) ‚Üí interfere with insulin signaling

Fat Distribution: Android (apple-shaped) obesity worse than gynoid (pear-shaped)

Mathematical Relationship: BMI = mass(kg)/height(m)¬≤; >25 overweight, >30 obese

Genetic Component (Pedigree Function)
Heritability: Type 2 diabetes has ~40% heritability

Polygenic Risk: Not single gene but combinations (TCF7L2, PPARG, KCNJ11)

Epigenetics: Lifestyle can trigger genetic predispositions

Age Correlation
Physiological Aging: Muscle mass decreases 3-8% per decade after 30 ‚Üí less glucose storage capacity

Metabolic Decline: Insulin sensitivity decreases ~0.7% yearly after maturity

Cumulative Damage: Oxidative stress accumulates in pancreatic cells


Pregnancy History

Gestational Diabetes: Temporary insulin resistance during pregnancy

Beta-Cell Stress Test: Pregnancy reveals hidden pancreatic weakness

Future Risk: GD history = 7x higher type 2 diabetes risk within 10 years


Insulin Levels

Compensatory Hyperinsulinemia: Early diabetes = high insulin trying to overcome resistance

Progression: Eventually insulin production declines

HOMA Model: Homeostasis Model Assessment (insulin √ó glucose/405)
